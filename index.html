💡 Search Tips for Better Results

Get the most out of every job search with these proven strategies.

🔀

Use OR for synonyms

Connect similar titles with OR — e.g. "Software Engineer" OR "Developer" — to cast a wider net.

🎯

Use AND to narrow down

Combine skills with AND — e.g. "Python" AND "Machine Learning" — to find roles that need both.

📅

Filter by date

Set "Past 24 hours" or "Past week" to see the freshest listings and apply before the crowd.

🌍

Try multiple job boards

Run the same query on LinkedIn, Indeed, and Glassdoor to avoid missing hidden opportunities.

📋

Copy & track your queries

Save your best-performing URLs to revisit daily — great for systematic job-hunting campaigns.

🏷️

Add location keywords

Include a city or "Remote" in the Location field to get results tailored to where you want to work.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about the Job Search Query Builder.

What is a Boolean job search? +
Boolean search uses operators like AND, OR, and NOT to combine keywords and give you precise, targeted results. This tool automates Boolean query creation so you can focus on applying, not on crafting search strings.
Which job boards are supported? +
The tool supports LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, Wellfound (AngelList), Monster, Dice, and Remote.co. More boards can be suggested via the GitHub issues page.
Is this tool free? +
Yes — completely free and open source. No registration, no hidden fees, no data collection. The tool runs entirely in your browser.
How does the search work? +
The tool builds a Google site-search URL using your keywords and selected filters. When you click "Search Now", it opens Google's results filtered to just the chosen job board — no scraping, no APIs, just a smarter Google search.
Why aren't the results from the job board's own search? +
Google indexes job boards deeply and often provides more flexible filtering than native search. Using "site:" queries surfaces listings that may not appear in the job board's own keyword filters.
