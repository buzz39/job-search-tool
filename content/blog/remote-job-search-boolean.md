Remote work is no longer a perk — it's a job requirement for millions of professionals. But finding *genuinely* remote roles buried among hybrid and on-site listings is a different challenge entirely. Companies label their jobs inconsistently: "remote," "work from home," "fully distributed," "anywhere" — all meaning roughly the same thing.

The fix? Boolean search strings that sweep up every variation so you never miss a match.

## Why Boolean Search Is Perfect for Remote Job Hunting

Job boards use keyword-matching to return results. If a posting says "work from home" but you searched "remote," you might miss it entirely. Boolean search lets you chain synonyms together with the **OR** operator, casting a wider net while still staying targeted.

A well-built query can filter by role, location preference, seniority level, and remote type — all in a single search. You can build these queries manually, or use a [Job Search Query Builder](/) to generate them instantly without memorizing any syntax.

## Core Boolean Operators You Need

Before diving into templates, here's a quick refresher:

- **AND** — both terms must appear: `"Python" AND "remote"`
- **OR** — either term works: `"remote" OR "work from home"`
- **NOT** — exclude a term: `NOT "hybrid"`
- **" "** — exact phrase match: `"fully remote"`
- **( )** — group terms: `("remote" OR "WFH") AND "engineer"`

## Boolean Strings for Popular Job Roles

### Software Engineers & Developers

```
("software engineer" OR "software developer" OR "full stack developer") 
AND ("remote" OR "work from home" OR "fully remote" OR "distributed") 
AND ("React" OR "Node.js" OR "Python")
```

### Product Managers

```
("product manager" OR "senior PM" OR "product lead") 
AND ("remote" OR "fully remote" OR "anywhere") 
NOT "on-site" NOT "hybrid"
```

### Marketing & Content Roles

```
("content strategist" OR "content writer" OR "digital marketer" OR "SEO specialist") 
AND ("remote" OR "work from home" OR "remote-first") 
AND ("B2B" OR "SaaS" OR "startup")
```

### Customer Success & Support

```
("customer success manager" OR "customer support" OR "account manager") 
AND ("remote" OR "fully remote" OR "WFH") 
AND ("SaaS" OR "tech" OR "software")
```

## Platform-Specific Tips

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn has a native "Remote" filter, but it's inconsistent. Combine it with Boolean in the keyword field:

```
("remote" OR "fully remote" OR "distributed team") AND "open to work"
```

You can also try [LinkedIn X-Ray Search](/blog/linkedin-xray-search) via Google to find profiles and job postings that LinkedIn's own search misses.

### Indeed

Indeed allows Boolean in its "What" field. Use quoted phrases and OR chains:

```
"remote" OR "work from home" OR "fully distributed"
```

Then set the location field to "Remote" for a double filter. For more Indeed-specific tactics, check out our guide on [Boolean Search Tips for Indeed](/blog/boolean-search-indeed).

### Google (X-Ray Search for Jobs)

Google indexes job postings that never appear on mainstream boards. Try:

```
site:greenhouse.io OR site:lever.co "software engineer" "remote" -"hybrid"
```

Or target company career pages:

```
site:careers.company.com "remote" "engineer" 2026
```

### Wellfound / AngelList

Startup jobs skew heavily remote. Use their built-in "Remote" filter plus keyword search:

```
("remote" OR "fully remote") AND ("Series A" OR "Series B") AND "engineer"
```

## Filtering Out Fake Remote Jobs

Not every "remote" listing is truly remote. Some gotchas to watch for:

- **"Remote in [State/Country]"** — geo-restricted, not global
- **"Remote initially"** — temporary, likely returning to office
- **"Remote-friendly"** — means some people are remote, not you necessarily

Add exclusions to your strings:

```
("remote" OR "work from home") NOT "remote in" NOT "must be located" NOT "hybrid" NOT "relocation"
```
