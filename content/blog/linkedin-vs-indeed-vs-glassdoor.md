With dozens of job boards available, most job seekers default to one or two platforms and miss out on opportunities elsewhere. The three giants — **LinkedIn**, **Indeed**, and **Glassdoor** — each have distinct strengths, and understanding those differences can significantly improve your job search results.

Let's break down each platform so you can decide where to focus your energy — or better yet, use our [Job Search Query Builder](/) to search all three simultaneously.

## LinkedIn Jobs: Best for Professional Networking

### Strengths
- **Networking integration** — Apply and immediately connect with hiring managers
- **Recruiter visibility** — Recruiters actively search LinkedIn for candidates
- **Company insights** — See mutual connections, company culture, and employee testimonials
- **Easy Apply** — One-click applications for many listings
- **Premium features** — InMail, applicant insights, and salary data (paid)

### Weaknesses
- **Premium paywall** — Many advanced features require LinkedIn Premium ($30-60/month)
- **Oversaturated** — Popular roles can get 200+ applications within hours
- **Algorithm bias** — LinkedIn's native search sometimes prioritizes sponsored listings

### Best For
- Professional and corporate roles
- Tech, finance, marketing, and consulting
- Mid-career to senior professionals
- Anyone who values networking alongside job searching

## Indeed: Best for Volume and Variety

### Strengths
- **Largest job database** — Indeed aggregates listings from thousands of company websites and other job boards
- **No account required** — Browse and search without signing up
- **Salary estimator** — Shows estimated salary ranges for listings that don't include them
- **Company reviews** — Built-in review system similar to Glassdoor
- **Resume upload** — Let employers find you

### Weaknesses
- **Lower quality listings** — Since it aggregates broadly, you'll encounter more spam and expired listings
- **Less networking** — No built-in professional network
- **Duplicate listings** — The same job may appear multiple times from different sources

### Best For
- Entry-level and hourly positions
- Blue-collar and service industry jobs
- Healthcare, education, and government roles
- Anyone who wants the broadest possible search

### Pro Tip
Indeed's native search can be clunky. Use Boolean search via Google: `site:indeed.com "Registered Nurse" AND "Remote"`. Generate this instantly with our [Query Builder](/).

## Glassdoor: Best for Company Research

### Strengths
- **Company reviews** — Detailed, anonymous reviews from current and former employees
- **Salary data** — Extensive salary reports by role, company, and location
- **Interview insights** — Real interview questions and experiences shared by candidates
- **Company photos** — See office environments and team culture
- **"Know Your Worth"** — Personalized salary estimate tool

### Weaknesses
- **Smaller job database** — Fewer listings than Indeed or LinkedIn
- **Review authenticity** — Some reviews may be biased or planted
- **Requires account** — Must sign up to access full reviews and salary data
- **Less useful for applying** — Better for research than application

### Best For
- Researching company culture before applying
- Salary negotiation preparation
- Understanding interview processes
- Mid-career professionals evaluating offers

Use Glassdoor for research and our [Job Search Query Builder](/) for finding the listings. Search with `site:glassdoor.com/job-listing` to target Glassdoor's job pages specifically.

## Other Job Boards Worth Considering

Don't stop at the big three. Depending on your industry:

- **Wellfound (AngelList)** — Startups and early-stage companies
- **Dice** — Technology and engineering roles
- **Remote.co** — Exclusively remote positions
- **Monster** — General job board with resume services
- **FlexJobs** — Vetted remote and flexible jobs (paid)
- **We Work Remotely** — Tech-focused remote jobs

## Which Should You Pay For?

- **LinkedIn Premium** — Worth it if you're actively job searching. The InMail credits and applicant insights provide a real edge. Cancel after you land the job.
- **Glassdoor Premium** — Not necessary for most users. The free tier provides enough salary and review data.
- **Indeed** — Completely free for job seekers. No premium tier needed.

## Final Verdict

There's no single "best" job board. The winning strategy is using the right platform for the right purpose:

1. **LinkedIn** for applying and networking
2. **Indeed** for discovering the most listings
